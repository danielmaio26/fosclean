Ozone as sanitiser

Ozone, formed by 3 atoms of oxygen, is one of the most powerful oxidizers known, meaning it is able to eliminate not only viruses, but also a wide range of other polluting microorganisms present in the air, not to mention the elimination of unpleasant odours.

It can be said that ozone has no limits when it comes to eliminating microorganisms, since it works on them on various levels. Ozone is effective in removing bacteria, viruses, protozoa, nematodes, fungi, cell aggregates, spores, and cysts. In addition, it acts at a lower concentration rate and with less contact time than other disinfectants.

In fact, according to WHO, ozone is the most efficient disinfectant for all types of microorganisms. Viruses, fungi, and bacteria (including antibiotic-resistant strains) are very vulnerable by nature when outside the host and eliminating them is relatively simple through ozone disinfecting treatments, capable of even removing their spores.
Ozone is especially useful for eliminating, among many others, the Ebola virus in the air. It has been shown that ozone is at least 10 times more powerful than Chlorine as a disinfectant. As aforementioned, according to WHO, ozone is the most efficient disinfectant for all types of microorganisms.

Therefore, the use of ozone, both in water and in the air for disinfecting rooms and surfaces is substantially preferable than the use of other disinfectants. Not only for its effectiveness but also because of its rapid decomposition, which leaves no hazardous waste.

As mentioned above, ozone can be used to disinfect and destroy molecules of viruses and bacteria. It is also effective at removing odours of all kinds, oxidizing their molecules. Ozone generators have always been useful, even more so now, proving to be an essential weapon in the fight against COVID-19 and the prevention of its contagion, especially in places where the risks are higher.
